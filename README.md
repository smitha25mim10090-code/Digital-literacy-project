Project Overview: The Student Digital Ambassador Role
I have been appointed as a Student Digital Ambassador, a role designed to bridge the gap between basic internet usage and professional digital competence. My primary objective is to build a Digital Literacy Portfolio that serves as a resource for my peers.
The project is structured around five core tasks that map directly to the course modules. All deliverables must be organized into a GitHub repository named digital-literacy-project. This repository acts as your live portfolio, while a final Project Report (100-150 words per task) documents my reflections, tools used, and key learnings 

Module 1: Digital Literacy Awareness & Visual Design

* Core Objective: Defining digital literacy and its significance in modern academia and professional life.
* Requirement: Create a high-quality infographic (using Canva, Prezi, or Miro) covering at least three key topics: digital tools for students, safe internet practices, professional online presence, or email etiquette (p. 3).
* Learning Outcome: Mastering browser-based design tools to communicate complex information visually and effectively.

Module 2: Professional Digital Presence & Portfolio Building

* Core Objective: Establishing a professional "digital footprint" that employers and internship coordinators can verify.
* Requirement: Set up or update accounts on at least three platforms: GitHub, LinkedIn, Kaggle, Stack Overflow, or ResearchGate. You must specifically create a GitHub profile README and update your LinkedIn education details (p. 3).
* Learning Outcome: Understanding the professional utility of different platforms and maintaining a minimal, professional profile without oversharing personal data.

Module 3: Coding Practice & Cloud Collaboration

* Core Objective: Navigating the tools required for technical practice and team-based academic projects.
* Requirement:
* Part A: Complete one beginner-level coding challenge (e.g., Python Introduction) on HackerRank, CodeChef, or HackerEarth.
   * Part B: Design a 5-question Google Form quiz on digital literacy, including both multiple-choice and short-answer questions
* Learning Outcome: Building familiarity with automated assessment platforms and cloud-based data collection tools.

Module 4: Professional Communication & Social Media Etiquette

* Focus: Bridging the gap between casual social messaging and professional correspondence.
* Requirement:
* Drafting Emails: Write two formal emails (one for an assignment extension and one for an internship inquiry) focusing on subject lines, tone, and structure.
   * Checklist: Create a 10-point Social Media Do's and Don'ts list specifically for college students (p. 5).
* Learning Outcome: Developing the ability to communicate with faculty and industry professionals with clarity and respect.

Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention

* Focus: Identifying and mitigating digital threats prevalent in the Indian digital ecosystem.
* Requirement: Research a case study on Phishing, UPI Fraud, Identity Theft, or Cyberbullying. You must also create a "Stay Safe Online" guide featuring at least 8 actionable tips and the official 1930 Helpline and National Cyber Crime Portal details (pp. 5-6).
* Learning Outcome: Developing a "security-first" mindset and learning the legal and procedural steps for reporting digital crimes.
